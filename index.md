## The Book: *Mercury*

- [[mercury-wilkins]] — Overview of the book: first English cryptology text (1641), its three-part structure (secrecy, swiftness, both joined), historical significance

## Chapter-by-Chapter

### Part 1: Secrecy (Chapters 1–14)

- [[ch-1-dependence-in-nature]] — Communication as natural necessity; angels vs humans; language not innate; writing as time-spanning; the Indian slave and the figs
- [[ch-2-secrecy-conditions]] — Two criteria for secrecy (hard to unfold, devoid of suspicion); cryptologia/cryptographia/semaeologia; fables and parables as hidden speech
- [[ch-3-speech-in-words]] — Inventing new words (canting, conjuring); changing known language by inversion, transmutation, diminution, augmentation
- [[ch-4-secret-conveyances]] — Physical concealment: messages in hare's belly (Harpagus), head-shaved servant (Histiaeus), swallowed gold (Jews at Jerusalem), underwater messengers, nets and barriers
- [[ch-5-materials]] — Secrecy in paper and ink: scytale, secret inks (sal ammoniac, onion juice, alum), knotted strings, the string-through-holes tablet (Aeneas Tacticus)
- [[ch-6-transposition]] — Transposition ciphers: the Spartan scytale, rearranging letters, the "Argyll cipher" principle
- [[ch-7-equal-letters-powers]] — Substitution ciphers: Albam and ATBASH (Hebrew cabalism), Caesar's D-for-A cipher, the key-character system (ancestor of Vigenère), Roman cipher use
- [[ch-8-more-letters]] — Writing with extra letters concealing the message; the "Trevanion cipher" principle (embedded text); Cardan grille; multiple alphabets
- [[ch-9-barbarous-words]] — Binary encoding: any 5 positions yield 32 combinations; double-alphabet system; how to express every letter with just two signs; the "best kind of secret writing"
- [[ch-10-fewer-letters]] — Abbreviations: Roman notae, Cicero's shorthand, Justinian's ban on abbreviated law books, speed vs secrecy trade-off
- [[ch-11-invented-characters]] — Geometric ciphers (points, lines, figures); frequency analysis method (vowels by frequency, single characters = A/I/O, common letter frequencies); the steganographic advantage of mathematical-looking messages
- [[ch-12-shorthand]] — Word-characters: Tyro's notes, Egyptian hieroglyphics, emblems as symbolic writing, the transition from letter-ciphers to concept-symbols
- [[ch-13-universal-character]] — Characters for things and notions directly; the vision of a language all nations can read; how this became Wilkins's *Essay* (1668)
- [[ch-14-signs-gestures]] — Semaeologia: ex congruo (natural signs), ex placito (arbitrary signs); Bede's finger-signing; Roman gesture systems; the suspicion problem

### Part 2: Swiftness (Chapters 15–16)

- [[ch-15-spiritual-substances]] — Angels as messengers; Trithemius's three Saturnine angels; the burning of his manuscript by Frederick II; sympathetic notification of distant friends' deaths
- [[ch-16-swiftness-by-bodies]] — Inanimate (arrows, bullets); animate: foot-runners (Lydas, Roman boy), beasts (horses, dromedaries, the wind-conceived mares of Lusitania), birds (carrier pigeons at Mutina, Taurosthenes's Olympic news)

### Part 3: Joining Secrecy and Swiftness (Chapters 17–20)

- [[ch-17-joining-secrecy-swiftness-sound]] — Sound signalling: anything with double difference can communicate; Xerxes's voice relay; Picts Wall pipes; Walchius's voice-storage fantasy (refuted); binary bell/cannon system; siege of Navarre
- [[ch-18-musical-notes]] — Musical alphabet: Godwin's lunar code cracked; vowels as minims on the stave; Gloria Deo soli in musical notation; universal language through music
- [[ch-19-fabulous-magical]] — Debunking: the sympathetic magnet needle fantasy (Strada, refuted by Cabaeus); Pythagoras writing on the moon; enchanted glasses; optical writing; actual range: 2–3 feet
- [[ch-20-polybius-torch-cipher]] — The Polybius 5×5 grid with 10 torches; Fortius's three-torch system; two-torch motion code (semaphore before semaphore); Chinese smoke signals; beacon multiplication

### Conclusion

- [[conclusion-moral-defense]] — Mercury as patron of thieves; knowledge cuts both ways (detection as well as deception); Cadmus's serpent teeth; Thamus rejecting writing; "we may as well cut out our tongues"

## Entities

- [[john-wilkins]] — Biography: 1614–1672, founder of Royal Society, Bishop of Chester, brother-in-law of Cromwell, author of *Mercury* and the *Essay*
- [[francis-godwin]] — 1562–1633, Bishop of Hereford, author of *The Man in the Moone* and *Nuntius Inanimatus* — the works that inspired *Mercury*
- [[polybius-cipher]] — The Polybius square: 5×5 grid coordinate cipher, 10-torch signalling system, survival into modern cryptography
- [[royal-society]] — The Royal Society of London (founded 1660): Wilkins as first secretary, experimental science, Wadham College origins
- [[leibniz]] — Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz (1646–1716): characteristica universalis, influenced by Wilkins's universal language
- [[trithemius]] — Johannes Trithemius (1462–1516): Steganographia, Polygraphia, the burned manuscript
- [[bacon-francis]] — Francis Bacon (1561–1626): "our English Aristotle," the Advancement of Learning, biliteral cipher
- [[porta-baptista]] — Giambattista della Porta (1535–1615): Magia Naturalis, cipher disk, the Navarre siege
- [[bede]] — The Venerable Bede (c.673–735): De Loquela per Gestum Digitorum, finger-signing
- [[josephus]] — Flavius Josephus (c.37–c.100): Jews swallowing gold at the Jerusalem siege
