## Chapter-by-Chapter

### Part 1: Secrecy (Chapters 1–14)

- [ch-1-dependence-in-nature](concepts/ch-1-dependence-in-nature.md) — Communication as natural necessity; angels vs humans; language not innate; writing as time-spanning; the Indian slave and the figs
- [ch-2-secrecy-conditions](concepts/ch-2-secrecy-conditions.md) — Two criteria for secrecy (hard to unfold, devoid of suspicion); cryptologia/cryptographia/semaeologia; fables and parables as hidden speech
- [ch-3-speech-in-words](concepts/ch-3-speech-in-words.md) — Inventing new words (canting, conjuring); changing known language by inversion, transmutation, diminution, augmentation
- [ch-4-secret-conveyances](concepts/ch-4-secret-conveyances.md) — Physical concealment: messages in hare's belly (Harpagus), head-shaved servant (Histiaeus), swallowed gold (Jews at Jerusalem), underwater messengers, nets and barriers
- [ch-5-materials](concepts/ch-5-materials.md) — Secrecy in paper and ink: scytale, secret inks (sal ammoniac, onion juice, alum), knotted strings, the string-through-holes tablet (Aeneas Tacticus)
- [ch-6-transposition](concepts/ch-6-transposition.md) — Transposition ciphers: the Spartan scytale, rearranging letters, the "Argyll cipher" principle
- [ch-7-equal-letters-powers](concepts/ch-7-equal-letters-powers.md) — Substitution ciphers: Albam and ATBASH (Hebrew cabalism), Caesar's D-for-A cipher, the key-character system (ancestor of Vigenère), Roman cipher use
- [ch-8-more-letters](concepts/ch-8-more-letters.md) — Writing with extra letters concealing the message; the "Trevanion cipher" principle (embedded text); Cardan grille; multiple alphabets
- [ch-9-barbarous-words](concepts/ch-9-barbarous-words.md) — Binary encoding: any 5 positions yield 32 combinations; double-alphabet system; how to express every letter with just two signs; the "best kind of secret writing"
- [ch-10-fewer-letters](concepts/ch-10-fewer-letters.md) — Abbreviations: Roman notae, Cicero's shorthand, Justinian's ban on abbreviated law books, speed vs secrecy trade-off
- [ch-11-invented-characters](concepts/ch-11-invented-characters.md) — Geometric ciphers (points, lines, figures); frequency analysis method (vowels by frequency, single characters = A/I/O, common letter frequencies); the steganographic advantage of mathematical-looking messages
- [ch-12-shorthand](concepts/ch-12-shorthand.md) — Word-characters: Tyro's notes, Egyptian hieroglyphics, emblems as symbolic writing, the transition from letter-ciphers to concept-symbols
- [ch-13-universal-character](concepts/ch-13-universal-character.md) — Characters for things and notions directly; the vision of a language all nations can read; how this became Wilkins's *Essay* (1668)
- [ch-14-signs-gestures](concepts/ch-14-signs-gestures.md) — Semaeologia: ex congruo (natural signs), ex placito (arbitrary signs); Bede's finger-signing; Roman gesture systems; the suspicion problem

### Part 2: Swiftness (Chapters 15–16)

- [ch-15-spiritual-substances](concepts/ch-15-spiritual-substances.md) — Angels as messengers; Trithemius's three Saturnine angels; the burning of his manuscript by Frederick II; sympathetic notification of distant friends' deaths
- [ch-16-swiftness-by-bodies](concepts/ch-16-swiftness-by-bodies.md) — Inanimate (arrows, bullets); animate: foot-runners (Lydas, Roman boy), beasts (horses, dromedaries, the wind-conceived mares of Lusitania), birds (carrier pigeons at Mutina, Taurosthenes's Olympic news)

### Part 3: Joining Secrecy and Swiftness (Chapters 17–20)

- [ch-17-joining-secrecy-swiftness-sound](concepts/ch-17-joining-secrecy-swiftness-sound.md) — Sound signalling: anything with double difference can communicate; Xerxes's voice relay; Picts Wall pipes; Walchius's voice-storage fantasy (refuted); binary bell/cannon system; siege of Navarre
- [ch-18-musical-notes](concepts/ch-18-musical-notes.md) — Musical alphabet: Godwin's lunar code cracked; vowels as minims on the stave; Gloria Deo soli in musical notation; universal language through music
- [ch-19-fabulous-magical](concepts/ch-19-fabulous-magical.md) — Debunking: the sympathetic magnet needle fantasy (Strada, refuted by Cabaeus); Pythagoras writing on the moon; enchanted glasses; optical writing; actual range: 2–3 feet
- [ch-20-polybius-torch-cipher](concepts/ch-20-polybius-torch-cipher.md) — The Polybius 5×5 grid with 10 torches; Fortius's three-torch system; two-torch motion code (semaphore before semaphore); Chinese smoke signals; beacon multiplication

### Conclusion

- [conclusion-moral-defense](concepts/conclusion-moral-defense.md) — Mercury as patron of thieves; knowledge cuts both ways (detection as well as deception); Cadmus's serpent teeth; Thamus rejecting writing; "we may as well cut out our tongues"
