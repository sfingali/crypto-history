# Notarikon

A Jewish cabalistic method of interpretation where each letter of a word is treated as the initial letter of another word, forming an acrostic. Reverse: forming a word from the initial letters of a phrase. Along with **Atbash** (reverse substitution) and **Albam** (halved substitution), Notarikon is one of the three cabalistic cipher methods described by Wilkins in [Chapter 7](concepts/ch-7-equal-letters-powers.md) of *Mercury*.

Wilkins presents these cabalistic ciphers as evidence that secret writing has ancient, even sacred, precedent — not a frivolous modern invention but a practice embedded in the Hebrew scriptures themselves. He cites the rabbinical tradition (*Ziruph* or *combinatio*) from which these methods derive.
