# History of Cryptography

A timeline of major developments in secret communication, as traced by John Wilkins in *Mercury* and extended by later scholarship.

## Classical Antiquity

- **Polybius** (2nd century BCE): 5×5 grid coordinate cipher; 10-torch signalling system → [polybius-cipher](entities/polybius-cipher.md)
- **Caesar** (1st century BCE): D-for-A shift cipher → [ch-7-equal-letters-powers](concepts/ch-7-equal-letters-powers.md)
- **Aeneas Tacticus** (4th century BCE): earliest known military cipher treatise; scytale, knotted strings → [ch-4-secret-conveyances](concepts/ch-4-secret-conveyances.md), [ch-5-materials](concepts/ch-5-materials.md)

## Hebrew Cabalism

- **ATBASH**, **Albam**, and **Notarikon** — methods embedded in the Hebrew scriptures, cited by Wilkins as evidence of cryptography's ancient roots → [atbash](concepts/atbash.md), [notarikon](concepts/notarikon.md)

## Medieval

- **Bede** (8th century): finger-signing system for numbers → [bede](entities/bede.md), [ch-14-signs-gestures](concepts/ch-14-signs-gestures.md)
- **Roger Bacon** (13th century): secret writing methods → [bacon-francis](entities/bacon-francis.md)
- **Albertus Magnus** (13th century): the speaking brazen head legend → [ch-17-joining-secrecy-swiftness-sound](concepts/ch-17-joining-secrecy-swiftness-sound.md)

## Renaissance

- **Trithemius** (1499–1518): *Steganographia* and *Polygraphia* — first printed cryptography texts → [trithemius](entities/trithemius.md)
- **Cardano** (1550): grille cipher → [cardan-grille](concepts/cardan-grille.md)
- **Porta** (1563): cipher disk, *De Furtivis Literarum Notis* → [porta-baptista](entities/porta-baptista.md), [porta-cipher-disk](concepts/porta-cipher-disk.md)
- **Bacon** (1623): biliteral cipher (first binary alphabet) → [bacon-cipher](concepts/bacon-cipher.md)

## 17th Century — The English School

- **Godwin** (1629/1638): *Nuntius Inanimatus* and *The Man in the Moone* — the fiction that inspired Wilkins → [francis-godwin](entities/francis-godwin.md)
- **Wilkins** (1641): *Mercury* — first English cryptology text; comprehensive survey of all known methods → [mercury-wilkins](entities/mercury-wilkins.md)
- **Wilkins** (1668): *Essay towards a Real Character* — universal language project → [universal-language-project](concepts/universal-language-project.md)
- **Royal Society** (1660): institutionalisation of experimental science → [royal-society](entities/royal-society.md)

## Post-Wilkins

- **Leibniz** (late 17th century): *characteristica universalis*, influenced by Wilkins → [leibniz](entities/leibniz.md)
- **Chappe** (1792): optical telegraph — mechanical realisation of Wilkins's torch relay concept
- **Morse** (1837): electrical telegraph — the binary alphabet becomes the foundation of modern communication
