# Chapter 10: Writing by Fewer Letters Than Required

The counterpart to Chapters 8-9, this brief chapter addresses encoding messages with **fewer letters** than their natural expression requires — the art of abbreviation and contraction for secrecy.

## Abbreviation in the Learned Languages

> "Abbreviations have been anciently used in all the learned languages, especially in common forms, and phrases of frequent use."

### Hebrew Contractions

- **וגו** for **וגומר** (*et totum illud*) — equivalent to *et cetera*, &c.
- **לפי** for **לפי שאמר** (*secundum dicere*) — equivalent to *viz.* or *v.g.*

### Greek Contractions

- **Χς** for **Χριστος**
- **Θς** for **Θεος**
- **ανου** for **ανθρωπου**

### Latin Contractions

- **Dns** for *Dominus*
- **aa** for *anima* (as noted in Chapter 3)

But these, Wilkins notes, were "rather for the speed of writing, than the secrecy" — their primary purpose was convenience, not concealment.

## Jewish Memorials: First-Letter Abbreviations

> "Sometimes words were expressed only by their first letters. Thus did the Jews write all their memorials, and common forms."

### The Origin of "Maccabee"

The most striking example: Judas Maccabeus got his name from this practice. Before battle against Antiochus, he gave the watch-word from Exodus 15:11:

> *"Who is like unto thee (O Lord) amongst the gods?"*

He inscribed on his ensigns the capital letters **מכבי** (*Maccabi*). "Whereupon after the victory, the soldiers stiled their captain by that name."

### The Rabbis on Mystical Meanings

The rabbis find mystical meanings in individual letters:
- Psalm 3:1, the word **רבים** (many rise against me) — each letter stands for a different nation: **ר** = Romans, **ב** = Babylonians, **י** = Ionians/Greeks, **ם** = Medes
- Genesis 49:10 (speaking of Shilo) — another rabbi reads the four peoples (Jews, Christians, Heathens, Turks) from the letters

### The Trinity in Genesis

From Genesis 1:1's **ברא** (created), the letters are parsed:
- **ב** (Beth) = **בן** (the Son)
- **ר** (Resh) = **רוח** (the Holy Ghost)
- **א** (Aleph) = **אב** (the Father)

"If you will believe the Jews, the holy spirit hath purposely involved in the words of scripture, every secret that belongs to any art or science, under such cabalisms as these."

### The Addition of He to Abraham and Sarah

Wilkins treats this more seriously: God's addition of **ה** to Abram → Abraham and Sarai → Sarah (Genesis 17) was "not without some secret mystery" — it is "the chief letter of the tetragrammaton," suggesting that among their posterity, they "should also be the parents of the Messias, who was Jehovah." He also notes Christ calling himself Alpha and Omega (Revelation 1:8) as support.

### Warning Against Excess

> "Though such conjectures may be allowable in some particulars, yet to make all scriptures capable of the like secrets, does give such a latitude to men's roving and corrupt fancies, as must needs occasion many wild and strange absurdities."

Wilkins cites Irenaeus, who noted that "from such idle collections as these, many heresies of the Valentinians and Gnostics had their first beginnings."

## Roman Siglae (Abbreviations)

### Common Examples

- **S. P. D.** = *Salutem plurimum dicit*
- **S. P. Q. R.** = *Senatus populusque Romanus*
- **C. R.** = *Civis Romanus*
- **U. C.** = *Urbs condita*

### Etymology and Usage

These single letters were called *siglae* (per syncopen, from obsolete *sigillae*, whence *sigillatim*). They were "inscribed in their coins, statues, arms, monuments, and public records."

Wilkins quotes Valerius Probus: the study of these is "very necessary for one that would understand the Roman affairs." They covered "names of curiae, tribes, immunities, priesthoods, powers, magistrates... sacred, civil, and military affairs, colleges, decuries, fasti, numbers, measures, civil law, and the like."

### Origin

First used by notaries at senates and public assemblies, then retained in statutes and civil laws. Manilius makes knowledge of them the mark of a good lawyer.

### Judicial Use

In Roman courts, judges cast small stones (*tesserae*) into an urn:
- **A** = absolve
- **K** (from Greek κατακρινω) = condemn
- **N. L.** (*non liquet*) = suspend judgment

### Justinian's Ban

> "Because of those many ambiguities which this contracted way of writing was liable unto, and the great inconveniences that might happen thereupon in the misinterpretation of laws; therefore the emperor Justinian did afterward severely forbid any further use of them."

From *Codex* 1.17.1-2: *"Neque enim licentiam aperimus ex tali codice in judicium aliquid recitari."* — We grant no permission for anything from such a codex to be read in court.

## Secrecy Potential

> "The chief purpose of these ancient abbreviations amongst the Romans, was properly for their speed. But it is easy to apprehend how by compact they may be contrived also for secrecy."

Custom abbreviations, agreed upon in advance between two parties, could serve cryptographic purposes — though this is a minor point compared to the elaborate systems of surrounding chapters.
